Module C

Measurement Model Assessment

This module assesses whether your indicators (survey items) measure your constructs correctly. In journal review, measurement model problems are one of the most common reasons for rejection.

0. The Correct Workflow (Do Not Skip)

  1. Run PLS-SEM Algorithm
  2. Check Reflective Measurement: Loadings → Reliability → AVE → Discriminant Validity
  3. If you have Formative Constructs: Weights → VIF → Significance (Bootstrapping)
  4. Only after measurement passes → go to Module D (Structural Model)
Rule
Never interpret structural paths if measurement is not valid.

1. Indicator Reliability (Outer Loadings)

Outer loading shows how strongly an indicator represents its construct. It is essentially the indicator’s “signal strength” for that latent variable.

Meaning (Not Just a Number)

A loading of 0.708 is special because it implies:

0.708² ≈ 0.50 → the construct explains ~50% of the indicator’s variance.

This is why 0.708 is often used as the target threshold.

Traffic Light Rules

Keep
Strong indicator
≥ 0.708
Review Carefully
Consider removing only if AVE/CR improves and content validity is not harmed.
0.40–0.70
Remove
Weak indicator; usually should be dropped
< 0.40

Do not delete items mechanically. Preserve theoretical meaning first.

SmartPLS 4 Steps

  1. Calculate → PLS-SEM Algorithm
  2. Go to Results → Outer Loadings
  3. Check indicators for each construct

You can also show loadings directly on the model view (top toolbar display options).

2. Internal Consistency Reliability

Reliability asks: do the indicators of a construct move together consistently? In PLS-SEM, we primarily report Composite Reliability (ρc) and often ρA.

Cronbach’s Alpha vs. Composite Reliability vs. rho_A

  • Cronbach’s Alpha (α): lower bound; assumes equal indicator quality
  • Composite Reliability (ρc): preferred; weights indicators by their loadings
  • rho_A (ρA): often recommended as a more accurate reliability estimate in PLS
Reporting Practice
Many Q1 papers report α, ρA, and ρc together for transparency.

Reliability Thresholds

Good
Suitable for most confirmatory research
0.70–0.95
Exploratory Acceptable
Sometimes acceptable for early-stage scales
0.60–0.70
Too High
May indicate redundancy (items are basically duplicates)
> 0.95

SmartPLS 4 Steps

  1. Run PLS-SEM Algorithm
  2. Results → Construct Reliability and Validity
  3. Read α, ρA, and ρc

3. Convergent Validity (AVE)

Convergent validity asks whether indicators of a construct truly converge to represent the same concept.

AVE Meaning

AVE indicates the proportion of indicator variance captured by the construct.

AVE = 0.60 → construct captures 60% signal, 40% is measurement error.

Threshold

AVE
Convergent validity established
≥ 0.50

SmartPLS 4 Steps

  1. Results → Construct Reliability and Validity
  2. Check AVE for each reflective construct

4. Discriminant Validity (HTMT + Evidence)

Discriminant validity ensures that constructs are empirically distinct. Modern PLS-SEM practice prioritizes HTMT.

Meaning

HTMT estimates how similar two constructs are. If HTMT is very high, the constructs may be measuring the same concept.

Thresholds

Conservative
Suitable for most fields
< 0.85
More Liberal
Sometimes accepted depending on domain
< 0.90

HTMT Inference (Bootstrapped CI)

Strong evidence is obtained when the bootstrapped confidence interval of HTMT does not include 1.

Best Evidence
HTMT CI does not include 1.00
CI ≠ 1

Many reviewers now prefer “HTMT inference” over relying only on a fixed cutoff.

SmartPLS 4 Steps

  1. Results → Discriminant Validity → HTMT
  2. For inference: run Bootstrapping
  3. Bootstrapping results → HTMT inference / confidence intervals

5. Formative Measurement Model (If You Have Formative Constructs)

Reflective and formative assessment are different. For formative constructs, you do not use outer loadings and AVE in the same way.

Formative Checklist

  • Collinearity: indicator VIF values
  • Indicator weights: contribution of each indicator
  • Significance: bootstrapped t-values/p-values for weights
  • Content validity: theoretical coverage is essential

Typical Rules

Formative VIF
Collinearity acceptable
< 3.3
Weights
If weight is non-significant but loading is high, consider keeping based on theory.

SmartPLS 4 Steps

  1. Run PLS-SEM Algorithm
  2. Results → Collinearity Statistics (VIF)
  3. Run Bootstrapping → check indicator weights

Measurement Model Completed

If your loadings, reliability, AVE, and HTMT are acceptable, you may proceed to hypothesis testing in Module D – Structural Model.
