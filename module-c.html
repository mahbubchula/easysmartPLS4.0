Module C

Measurement Model Assessment

The "Outer Model". We check if our survey questions (indicators) are accurately measuring the latent variables (constructs).

1. Indicator Reliability

Does a specific survey question belong to the construct it's assigned to?

Outer Loadings

The Outer Loading is a correlation value (0 to 1) showing how strongly an indicator is connected to its construct.

Construct: "Quality"
| (0.85)
Question 1: "The site is fast"

Analogy: If you throw a ball at a target, how close did it land to the bullseye? High loading = Close to bullseye.

The Traffic Light Rules

Acceptable
The question is a strong measure.
> 0.708
Exploratory (Check carefully)
Between 0.40 and 0.70. Keep only if deleting it increases AVE/Composite Reliability.
0.4 - 0.7
Delete
The question does not belong here. Remove it from the model.
< 0.40

How to get this in SmartPLS 4

  1. Run the algorithm (Run -> PLS-SEM Algorithm).
  2. Go to the Final Results tab on the right side.
  3. Click Construct Reliability/Validity.
  4. Look at the first table: Outer Loadings.

Pro Tip: To see loadings graphically on the model, click the calculation button (small calculator icon) on the top toolbar and select "Path Coefficients" or "Outer Loadings" view.

2. Internal Consistency Reliability

Do all the questions for a construct measure the same thing consistently?

Why not Cronbach's Alpha?

Traditional statistics use "Cronbach's Alpha". However, PLS-SEM prefers Composite Reliability (rho_c).

  • Cronbach's Alpha: Assumes all indicators are equally important ("Tau-equivalent"). This is often unrealistic.
  • Composite Reliability: Doesn't assume equal importance. It acknowledges that some questions might be better measures than others.

Thresholds for Consistency

Composite Reliability
The construct is internally consistent.
> 0.70

For exploratory research, values between 0.60 and 0.70 are sometimes accepted.

3. Convergent Validity

Does the construct explain a large amount of variance in its indicators?

Average Variance Extracted (AVE)

AVE tells you: "On average, how much of the information in the questions is captured by the construct?"

If AVE is low, it means your questions are "noisy" or measuring random error, not the construct.

Construct captures 60% of the info (AVE = 0.60)
40% is just Error/Noise

The 50% Rule

AVE
Convergent Validity is established.
> 0.50

Meaning: The construct explains at least 50% of the variance in its indicators.

4. Discriminant Validity

Is this construct statistically different from the other constructs in the model?

Heterotrait-Monotrait (HTMT) Ratio

This is the modern, gold-standard test for discriminant validity in PLS-SEM.

Old Method (Fornell-Larcker): Comparing square root of AVE to correlations. (Now considered too liberal).

New Method (HTMT): Checks if the correlation between two constructs is significantly different from 1.

Intuition
If HTMT is high (near 1.0), the two constructs are basically the same thing. You must merge them or remove one.

HTMT Thresholds

Conservative
Very safe threshold for most journals.
< 0.85
Relaxed (Henseler et al.)
Acceptable if 0.85 is too strict for your field.
< 0.90

Note: The HTMT value should be compared to 1. Values above 1.0 clearly indicate a lack of discriminant validity.

Measurement Model Complete!

If your Loadings > 0.7, AVE > 0.5, and HTMT < 0.85...
Congratulations! Your survey is valid.
