Research Design & Theory

Building the foundation: From abstract ideas to a testable conceptual framework.

The Conceptual Framework

Before you open SmartPLS, you must define your Constructs and Relationships. A theory is a logical explanation of why two variables might be related.

In PLS-SEM, we distinguish between:

  • Reflective Constructs: The indicators cause the construct (e.g., "Questions cause Satisfaction").
  • Formative Constructs: The construct causes the indicators (e.g., "Socio-economic status causes Income and Education").
[Visual: Theoretical Framework Diagram]

Key Definitions

Latent Variable (LV): A concept that cannot be measured directly (like "Trust" or "Loyalty").
Indicator (Item): The survey questions used to measure the LV.

Real World Example: E-Commerce

Let's imagine we are studying why customers buy products online.

Research Question: Does "Website Quality" impact "Customer Satisfaction"?

Variable Setup

Independent Variable (IV): Website Quality

Dependent Variable (DV): Customer Satisfaction

Hypothesis: H1: Website Quality has a positive significant effect on Customer Satisfaction.

Note: In SmartPLS, the arrow points from Website Quality (IV) to Satisfaction (DV).

SmartPLS 4.0 Manual

How to create your model in the software.

Step 1: Open SmartPLS and create a new project.
Step 2: Right-click the project -> Add Excel Data -> Import your survey data.
Step 3: Switch to the "Modeling" view.

Creating Variables

1. Drag a Latent Variable onto the canvas.
2. Rename it (e.g., "WebQual").
3. Drag an Indicator onto the canvas.
4. Connect the Indicator to the Latent Variable.
