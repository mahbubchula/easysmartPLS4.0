Module A

Research Design & Theory Building

This module establishes the conceptual and theoretical backbone of your research. Weak theory leads to rejection — even if SmartPLS results are perfect.

1. Why Research Design Comes Before Software

SmartPLS is a statistical testing tool, not a thinking tool. It cannot fix unclear theory, poor construct definition, or weak hypotheses.

Common Mistake
Running SmartPLS first and thinking later.
❌
Correct Logic
Theory → Model → Hypotheses → Data → SmartPLS
✔

2. What Exactly Is a Research Model?

A research model is a simplified representation of reality, built from theory, prior literature, and logical reasoning.

Measurement Model

Defines how abstract concepts (e.g., trust, satisfaction) are measured using observed indicators.

Evaluated using loadings, CR, AVE, HTMT.

Structural Model

Defines how constructs influence each other through hypotheses.

Evaluated using β, t-values, R², f², Q².

Think of the model as a **map** — SmartPLS only checks whether the map is correct.

3. Reflective vs. Formative Constructs (Critical Decision)

This decision determines how SmartPLS evaluates your model. A wrong choice leads to incorrect conclusions.

Reflective Constructs

  • Construct → Indicators
  • Indicators are interchangeable
  • High inter-correlation expected

Example: Satisfaction, Trust, Intention

Formative Constructs

  • Indicators → Construct
  • Indicators are not interchangeable
  • Low correlation is acceptable

Example: Service Quality, Socioeconomic Status

❌ Treating Service Quality as reflective without justification
✔ Most journal reviewers expect **explicit justification** for formative constructs

4. Writing Strong Hypotheses

Hypotheses translate theory into testable statements. Weak hypotheses are a major reason for desk rejection.

Rules for Good Hypotheses

  • Direction must be specified
  • Relationship must be causal
  • Based on literature or theory
Good Hypothesis
H1: Perceived Usefulness has a positive effect on Adoption Intention.
Bad Hypothesis
Perceived Usefulness is related to Adoption Intention.

5. Mini Research Case (Transportation Example)

Study Context

Understanding commuters’ adoption of low-carbon public transport in developing cities.

Construct Design

  • Perceived Usefulness (Reflective)
  • Environmental Concern (Reflective)
  • Service Quality (Formative)
  • Adoption Intention (Reflective)

Hypothesis Structure

H1: Perceived Usefulness → Adoption Intention (+)
H2: Environmental Concern → Adoption Intention (+)
H3: Service Quality → Perceived Usefulness (+)
H4: Service Quality → Adoption Intention (+)

This structure aligns with Q1 journals in transportation, sustainability, and information systems.
