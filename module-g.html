Research Output & Publication

Converting SmartPLS results into a journal-ready manuscript. Formatting tables, figures, and writing the discussion.

1. Reporting Tables

Reviewers need to see your numbers in a clear, standard format (usually APA style).

Table 1: The Validity Table

Combine Loadings, Reliability, AVE, and Discriminant Validity (HTMT) into one clean table or split them into two.

Construct Loadings CR AVE HTMT
Sat_1 0.825 0.892 0.675 -
Sat_2 0.801
Sat_3 0.845

*Use Bold formatting for values that meet the threshold. Add notes at the bottom indicating thresholds (e.g., "Loadings > 0.70").

Report the path coefficients, t-statistics, p-values, and decision (Supported/Not Supported).

2. The Path Model Figure

A picture is worth a thousand words. Your final figure should show the significant relationships.

SmartPLS 4.0 Export

  1. In the modeling view, use the Selection Tool to select the entire model (or press Ctrl+A).
  2. Right-click on the selected model.
  3. Choose Copy to Clipboard or Save as Graphics...
  4. Recommended Format: EMF (Vector) or PNG.

Use EMF for Microsoft Word (it scales without pixelating). Use PNG for web.

Polishing the Figure

  • Significance Levels: Use stars (*) on the arrows to denote significance (* p < 0.05, ** p < 0.01).
  • Remove Clutter: Hide insignificant paths (make them dashed or gray) to highlight the key findings.
  • Labeling: Ensure all Latent Variables are legible (avoid overlapping text).
  • R²: Add the R² value above the dependent variables (circles).

3. Writing the Discussion

This is where you earn your publication. Don't just repeat the numbers; explain them.

Template for Reporting a Hypothesis

Use this structure for every hypothesis:

H1 proposed that Website Quality has a positive effect on Customer Satisfaction.

As shown in Table 2, the results support H1 (β = 0.45, t = 5.61, p < 0.001). This indicates that higher website quality leads to significantly higher customer satisfaction levels.

Template for Discussion

Comparison: "Our findings align with Smith (2020) who also found a strong link between quality and satisfaction..."

Explanation: "The reason why quality impacts satisfaction so strongly is that users equate smooth design with trust..."

Implications:

  • Theoretical: "This study extends the DeLone & McLean model to include..."
  • Practical/Managerial: "Managers should prioritize server speed (Quality) over aesthetics to boost satisfaction."

Limitations: "The study was limited to [Country] and used a cross-sectional design..."
