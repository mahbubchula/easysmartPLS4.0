Module G

Research Output, Writing & Publication

This module teaches how to transform SmartPLS results into a complete, journal-ready manuscript that satisfies reviewers, editors, and academic standards.

0. What Reviewers Expect from PLS-SEM Papers

  • Transparent reporting of measurement and structural models
  • Clear tables with thresholds explicitly stated
  • Theoretical interpretation (not only statistics)
  • Managerial / policy implications
  • Limitations and future research directions
Key Mindset
Numbers support theory ‚Äî they do not replace it.

1. Reporting Tables (APA-Style)

Measurement Model Table

Report indicator loadings, reliability, AVE, and discriminant validity. Clearly state threshold values in table notes.

Advanced Analysis Tables

  • Mediation: Direct, Indirect, Total Effects
  • Moderation: Interaction Term (Œ≤, t, p)
  • PLSpredict: RMSE / MAE (PLS vs LM)
  • MGA: Group differences and p-diff

2. Path Model Figure

What the Final Figure Should Show

  • Significant paths (solid lines)
  • Insignificant paths (dashed or omitted)
  • Path coefficients (Œ≤)
  • R¬≤ values for endogenous constructs
Use vector formats (EMF / SVG) for journal submission.

3. Writing the Results Section

Golden Rule

The Results section reports findings ‚Äî interpretation belongs in the Discussion.

‚ÄúH1 was supported (Œ≤ = 0.45, t = 5.61, p < 0.001), indicating a significant positive effect of X on Y.‚Äù

Do not explain *why* here ‚Äî save that for the Discussion.

4. Writing the Discussion

Recommended Structure

  1. Restate key findings (briefly)
  2. Compare with prior literature
  3. Theoretical contributions
  4. Managerial / policy implications
  5. Limitations
  6. Future research directions
‚ÄúContrary to prior studies, price did not significantly affect satisfaction, suggesting that in digital services, perceived quality dominates cost considerations.‚Äù

5. Limitations & Future Research

  • Cross-sectional data
  • Single country / context
  • Self-reported measures
  • Model boundary conditions
Tip
Reviewers respect honest limitations ‚Äî hiding them harms credibility.

6. Journal Submission Strategy

  • Match theory + method to journal scope
  • Follow author guidelines exactly
  • Use reviewer-friendly tables
  • Respond politely and systematically to reviews
